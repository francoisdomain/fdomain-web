Everyone is a Leader

Leadership is not something you learn ; it's something you stumble across.

How many times have you heard (or told yourself), "I'm not a leader"?

We've been conditioned to believe that leadership is a title you earn, a skill you acquire, or a role you step into. Newsflash: it isn't.

Leadership isn't something you learn—it's something you stumble upon.

The moment you find something you believe in so deeply that you can't not act—that's leadership.

Can you lead without inspiring?

You can inspire without leading - but can you lead without inspiring?

And here comes the buzzword: VISION.

No word has been more overused—or misunderstood. "I want to increase my bottom line by 40%" has very little to do with Vision—it's merely an objective.

The distinction is the same as management vs. inspiration.

Leadership, however, is impossible without inspiration.

Licence to Lead

What gives you the licence to lead?

Leadership is not given; it's taken. But what gives you the right to take it?

The answer lies in conviction, not competence.

When your beliefs about what needs to be done outweigh your fears of stepping forward, you've earned your licence to lead.
